Crawlspace Encapsulation in Metro Atlanta

Vapor barriers, drainage, and dehumidification that turn a damp crawlspace into a clean, dry, protected part of your home.

The Room You Never See Affects Every Room You Live In

In many metro Atlanta homes, a significant share of the air on the first floor rises up from the crawlspace. When that crawlspace is damp, the air carrying moisture, mold spores, and musty odors moves straight into your living space. A wet crawlspace also feeds moisture into floor joists and subflooring, leading to sagging floors, wood rot, and pest problems over time.

Encapsulation seals the crawlspace off from ground moisture and outside humidity, then conditions the space so it stays dry permanently.

What Encapsulation Includes

  • Heavy-duty vapor barrier sealed across the ground and up foundation walls
  • Sealing of vents and outside air leaks
  • Drainage and sump installation where standing water is present
  • Crawlspace dehumidification sized for the space
  • Remediation of any existing mold on framing before sealing
  • Inspection of insulation and repair recommendations

Signs Your Crawlspace Needs Attention

Musty odors on the first floor, cupping or sagging hardwood floors, sweating ductwork, visible standing water after rain, or mold on floor joists all point to a crawlspace moisture problem. If a home inspector flagged your crawlspace during a sale, an evaluation will tell you exactly how serious it is.

Done in the Right Order

Encapsulating over an existing mold or water problem seals the problem in. If your crawlspace has active growth or standing water, we remediate and establish drainage first, then encapsulate. You get one plan covering the full sequence, with a written price for each stage.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will encapsulation lower my energy bills?+
Many homeowners see reduced heating and cooling costs because the HVAC system no longer fights humid crawlspace air, and sealed ductwork in a conditioned space performs more efficiently.
How long does encapsulation take?+
Most residential crawlspaces are completed in one to three days, depending on size and whether drainage or remediation is needed first.
Is a vapor barrier alone enough?+
A ground cover helps, but without sealing outside air leaks and controlling humidity, moisture still condenses on framing. Full encapsulation addresses all three sources: ground moisture, outside air, and humidity.
