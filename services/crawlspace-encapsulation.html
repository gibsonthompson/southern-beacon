Crawlspace Encapsulation in Metro Atlanta

A damp crawlspace supplies air to every room above it. We seal it, drain it, and dry it so it stops being the source.

The Stack Effect Is Not a Theory

Air moves upward through a house. Warm air rises and escapes through the upper levels, and the air replacing it gets pulled from the lowest point in the building, which in a large share of metro Atlanta homes is a vented dirt crawlspace. That means the humidity, the musty odor, and the spores under your floor are being delivered into your living space continuously.

This is why crawlspace work belongs to a mold company. Homeowners call us about a smell on the first floor or air quality symptoms with no visible growth anywhere, and the source turns out to be a space they have not opened in years.

What Encapsulation Includes

  • Removal of existing debris, failed insulation, and contaminated material
  • Remediation of active growth on joists and subfloor before anything gets sealed in
  • Heavy-duty reinforced vapor barrier across the ground and up the foundation walls, seams sealed
  • Sealing of foundation vents and outside air infiltration points
  • Drainage and sump installation where standing water or seepage is present
  • A dedicated dehumidifier sized to the volume, with drainage to daylight or a pump
  • Insulation assessment and correction of the ductwork and plumbing conditions driving condensation

Signs Yours Needs Attention

Musty odor on the first floor that you cannot trace. Cupping, sagging, or springy hardwood. Sweating ductwork or condensation on plumbing. Standing water after rain. Visible growth on floor joists. Insulation hanging down, which usually means it absorbed moisture and lost its grip. A home inspector flagging the crawlspace during a sale.

Sequence Matters

Encapsulating over an active moisture problem seals the problem in and gives it a stable, protected environment. If there is standing water, active growth, or a plumbing leak under there, that gets corrected first. We will tell you honestly if your crawlspace needs remediation and drainage before encapsulation is worth spending money on.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will encapsulation lower my energy bills?+
Many homeowners see a reduction, because the HVAC system stops conditioning humid crawlspace air and ductwork in a sealed space performs closer to its rating. We would not sell it on energy savings alone, but it is a real secondary benefit.
How long does it take?+
Most residential crawlspaces take one to three days. Add time if remediation or drainage work is needed first.
Is a plastic ground cover enough?+
A ground cover helps with soil evaporation but does nothing about outside air infiltration or the humidity already in the space. Without sealing and dehumidification, moisture still condenses on framing.
