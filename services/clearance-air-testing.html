Proof the Job Is Actually Done

When a remediation project ends, the space can look spotless while the air still carries elevated spore levels from incomplete work or breached containment. Clearance testing is how you know the difference. Air samples collected after remediation are analyzed by an accredited laboratory and compared against outdoor baseline levels. If the numbers are back to normal, the job is done. If they are not, the contractor goes back to work before containment comes down.

We include clearance testing on every remediation project we perform, and we also provide independent clearance testing for work done by other contractors.

What Clearance Testing Includes

  • Air samples collected inside the remediated area before containment is removed
  • Outdoor baseline samples for comparison
  • Analysis by an accredited laboratory
  • A written pass or fail determination with the supporting data
  • Documentation suitable for insurance, real estate transactions, and your records

Why Independent Verification Matters

A remediation invoice says work was performed. A clearance report says the work succeeded. That distinction matters when you are settling an insurance claim, selling your home, resolving a landlord-tenant dispute, or simply deciding whether your family can move back into a bedroom. The laboratory data removes the argument.

Testing Work Done by Others

If another company performed your remediation and you want independent confirmation before final payment or before containment comes down, we provide third-party clearance testing with no involvement in the original work. You receive the same accredited lab analysis and written determination.

Frequently Asked Questions

When should clearance testing happen?+
After remediation work is complete but before containment barriers are removed. Testing after barriers come down cannot isolate the work area.
What happens if the test fails?+
The report identifies the elevated levels, and the remediation contractor addresses the remaining contamination before re-testing. On our own projects, re-work and re-testing are on us.
How fast are results available?+
Standard laboratory turnaround is two to three business days, with expedited options when a closing or move-in date requires faster answers.
