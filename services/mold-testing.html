You Cannot Identify Mold by Looking at It

There are more than one hundred thousand known mold species, and only a few dozen commonly grow indoors. Color tells you almost nothing. The black staining on a bathroom ceiling is frequently a harmless surface mildew, while a genuinely aggressive species can appear white, olive, or barely visible at all. Species identification requires laboratory analysis under a microscope.

Testing answers three questions a visual inspection cannot: what is present, at what concentration, and whether the indoor environment differs meaningfully from the outdoor air surrounding the building.

How We Sample

  • Spore trap air cassettes drawn at a calibrated flow rate, capturing both viable and non-viable spores, since dead spores still carry allergenic proteins
  • Outdoor reference samples collected the same day, because indoor results are meaningless without the outdoor baseline to compare against
  • Surface sampling by tape lift or swab where visible growth needs species identification
  • Cavity sampling when contamination is suspected inside a wall or ceiling assembly
  • Chain-of-custody documentation from collection through laboratory receipt
  • Analysis by an accredited independent laboratory, not an in-house reading

Reading the Results Correctly

There is no federal or state threshold that defines a safe indoor spore count, which is why any company quoting you an official limit is inventing one. The accepted approach compares indoor samples to the outdoor baseline. In a normal building, the indoor spore types mirror the outdoors at equal or lower concentrations. A problem shows up as amplification: a species present indoors far above outdoor levels, or a species that does not belong in the outdoor profile at all.

Certain findings carry more weight than raw counts. Chaetomium and Stachybotrys are water-damage organisms that generally indicate sustained saturation rather than ordinary humidity, and finding either changes the conversation regardless of how modest the numbers look.

Results and Turnaround

Standard laboratory turnaround is two to three business days. Expedited analysis is available when a closing date, a move-in, or a health concern will not wait. You receive the raw laboratory report along with our written interpretation in plain language, and the report belongs to you to use however you need.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I always need testing?+
No. When mold is visible and the water source is obvious, testing often confirms what everyone already knows. We recommend it when the source is hidden, when species identification matters for health reasons, for real estate and legal documentation, and for verifying completed remediation.
What does testing cost?+
Cost depends on how many samples the situation calls for. We quote the number of samples and the price before collecting anything, so there is never a surprise laboratory charge.
Can a test prove my health symptoms come from mold?+
No test can establish medical causation. Testing documents what is in the building. Whether that exposure explains your symptoms is a question for your physician, and we are happy to provide the report for them to review.
