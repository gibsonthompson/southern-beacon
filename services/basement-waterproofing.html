Basement Waterproofing in Metro Atlanta

Wet basements grow mold. We stop the water at the source with drainage engineered for Georgia clay, backed by a written warranty.

Why a Mold Company Does Waterproofing

Mold is the symptom. Water is the disease. We kept getting called back to basements we had already remediated because someone else was supposed to fix the drainage and never did, so we brought the water work in house. If we remove growth from a foundation wall and the same wall takes on water next spring, we did not solve your problem.

Georgia sits on dense red clay that absorbs slowly and holds water against your foundation long after the rain stops. That saturated soil creates hydrostatic pressure, and pressure pushes moisture through block, mortar joints, floor seams, and cracks. It is a physics problem, and sealing paint on the inside of a wall does not solve a physics problem.

Solutions We Install

  • Interior drainage systems that collect water at the footing and route it to a sump before it reaches your floor
  • Sump pump installation, including battery backup for the storms that take your power out
  • Foundation crack repair and wall sealing where the assembly warrants it
  • Vapor barriers on foundation walls to stop diffusion through masonry
  • Exterior grading correction and downspout extension, often the cheapest fix and the most overlooked
  • Dehumidification sized to the space to hold humidity below the level that supports growth

Designed for Your Foundation

Every house takes on water differently. Before we recommend anything we evaluate where water is actually entering, how your lot sheds runoff, what your gutters and grading are doing, and what the walls themselves are telling us. The estimate explains each component and why it is there, so you are never buying a package that includes work your house does not need.

Waterproofing and Mold Together

A basement that has been damp for years usually has growth to address as well. Because we handle both, we sequence them correctly: remediate the existing contamination, correct the water, then verify. One contractor, one plan, and nobody pointing fingers about whose scope the leak belonged to.
