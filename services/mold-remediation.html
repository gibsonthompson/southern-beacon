Professional Mold Removal, Done Right

Finding mold in your home is unsettling, and the internet is full of conflicting advice about what to do next. Here is the reality: small surface mold on non-porous materials can sometimes be cleaned by a homeowner, but growth that covers a larger area, keeps returning, or has gotten into drywall, insulation, or framing needs professional remediation. Improper cleanup spreads spores through the air and into your HVAC system, turning a contained problem into a whole-house problem.

Southern Beacon Environmental provides complete mold remediation for homes and businesses throughout metro Atlanta. Every project follows industry-standard protocols from initial containment through final clearance testing, and every project includes correcting the moisture source, because removing mold without fixing the water only schedules the next outbreak.

What's Included in Every Remediation

  • Sealed containment with poly barriers and negative air pressure to protect the rest of your home
  • HEPA air filtration running throughout the project
  • Safe removal and disposal of contaminated drywall, insulation, and other affected materials
  • Antimicrobial treatment of remaining surfaces and framing
  • Identification and repair of the moisture source that caused the growth
  • Post-remediation clearance air testing through an accredited laboratory
  • Complete photo documentation for your records and insurance

Why the Moisture Source Matters

Mold cannot grow without moisture. Every remediation we perform includes finding where that moisture came from, whether it is a plumbing leak, foundation seepage, roof intrusion, or condensation from poor ventilation. Because we also handle waterproofing and drainage, we can correct the underlying problem on the same project instead of referring you to a second contractor.

How You Know It Worked

When the work is complete, we do not ask you to take our word for it. Independent air samples are collected and analyzed by an accredited laboratory, and you receive the results showing spore levels have returned to normal. That documentation protects you with your insurance company, and it protects the value of your home if you ever sell.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to leave my home during remediation?+
In most cases, no. Containment barriers and negative air pressure keep the work area isolated from the rest of the home. For larger projects or if household members have significant health sensitivities, we will discuss the best plan during your inspection.
How long does mold remediation take?+
Small, contained projects are often completed in one to two days. Larger projects involving demolition and structural drying can take several days to a week. You receive a clear timeline with your written estimate.
Is the mold inspection really free?+
Yes. We inspect, explain what we found, and provide a written estimate at no cost. If remediation is not necessary, we will tell you that as well.
