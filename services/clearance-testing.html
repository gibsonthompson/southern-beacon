Clearance Testing & Verification

The step that proves remediation worked: visual inspection, moisture verification, and accredited laboratory sampling before containment comes down.

Call (470) 760-5249 Book a Free Walk-Through

An Invoice Is Not Proof

Post-remediation verification, sometimes called clearance testing or PRV, is the step that determines whether a project actually succeeded. A work area can look immaculate while spore counts remain elevated from incomplete cleaning or a containment breach. Without verification there is no objective record that the space was returned to normal, which matters for your insurer, for a future sale, and for your own confidence in moving back into the room.

Timing Is Not Optional

Clearance is performed after remediation is complete but before containment barriers come down. Once the barriers are removed, the work area is no longer isolated and a sample can no longer tell you anything meaningful about it. Any company that tests after teardown has produced a document, not a verification.

What We Verify

  • Visual inspection confirming no remaining visible growth, dust, or debris in the work area
  • Moisture readings confirming materials have reached dry standard, generally at or below sixteen percent in wood
  • Confirmation that the moisture source was corrected, not merely dried
  • Spore trap air samples collected inside the former work area
  • Outdoor reference samples taken the same day for comparison
  • Surface sampling where Stachybotrys or similar sedimenting species were part of the original scope
  • Accredited independent laboratory analysis and a written pass or fail determination

What Passing Actually Means

A passing result means the work area has been returned to Condition 1, normal fungal ecology: no visible growth, materials dried to standard, and airborne spore types and concentrations comparable to or below the outdoor reference. If a sample fails, the remaining contamination is identified, the area is re-cleaned, and it is re-tested. On our own projects, re-work and re-testing after a failed clearance are ours to absorb, not yours.

Independence and Why We Say It Out Loud

Best practice holds that the party verifying remediation should have no financial stake in the outcome. Some states codify this. Florida law prohibits a company from assessing and remediating the same property within twelve months. Georgia has no such rule, which means a company here can find the mold, sell the removal, and certify its own work with nobody checking.

We think you should know that, including about us. Every sample we collect goes to an accredited independent laboratory that has no interest in the result, and on any project where you want a fully independent assessor to perform the clearance rather than us, we will work with the assessor of your choosing and provide access to the containment. We also perform third-party clearance testing on remediation completed by other contractors.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you test work another company performed?+
Yes. Third-party clearance on someone else's remediation is a service we provide, and it is worth doing before you release final payment or allow reconstruction to begin.
How fast do results come back?+
Standard laboratory turnaround is two to three business days, with expedited options when a closing or move-in date requires it.
What happens if it fails?+
The report identifies what failed and why. The area is re-cleaned and re-tested. When the remediation was ours, correcting a failed clearance is our cost.
