Field Checklist 01

Sound familiar? Your house is trying to tell you something.

Mold and water damage almost never announce themselves. They show up as small, easy-to-ignore signals, and by the time they're obvious, the problem behind the wall is usually months old.

If you checked even one box in your head reading this list, an inspection is worth your time. Ours is free, and we'll tell you the truth about what we find, even when the truth is "you don't need us."

SBE Inspection · Common FindingsMetro ATL
  • A musty, earthy smell in the basement, crawlspace, or closets that never quite goes away
  • Dark spots or discoloration on walls, ceilings, grout, or around vents
  • Water in the basement after heavy Georgia rain, even just a damp line along the wall
  • Allergies or asthma acting up at home but improving when you leave
  • Peeling paint, warped floors, or bubbling drywall
  • A home inspection report flagged mold, moisture, or "evidence of water intrusion"
  • White chalky residue (efflorescence) on basement block or concrete walls

Straight Answers

Questions we get every week.

We answer the phone 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. For active water emergencies in metro Atlanta we dispatch immediately. For mold inspections and waterproofing estimates, we typically schedule within 24 to 48 hours.

Some molds, including Stachybotrys (often called black mold), can aggravate allergies, asthma, and other respiratory issues, especially in children, older adults, and anyone with a compromised immune system. The honest answer is that you cannot identify a mold species by color alone. Testing tells you what you are dealing with, and remediation with proper containment removes it safely.

Surface cleaning often makes things worse. Wiping or spraying visible mold can release spores into the air and spread them through your home, and it does nothing about the moisture source that caused the growth. Proper remediation means containment, HEPA air filtration, removing affected materials, and fixing the water problem so it does not come back.

It depends on the cause. Mold resulting from a sudden, covered water event (like a burst pipe) is often covered. Mold from long-term leaks, humidity, or groundwater seepage usually is not. We document everything we find with photos, moisture readings, and reports, which gives you the strongest possible position with your carrier.

Small, contained problems can run a few hundred dollars. Larger jobs involving demolition, structural drying, and clearance testing cost more. We give you a written, line-item scope after inspection, so you know exactly what you are paying for and why, before any work starts.

We verify our own work. After remediation we perform clearance testing, including air sampling, and provide you with the results. You get documented proof that the air in your home is back to normal, not just our word for it.

Removal usually refers to wiping out visible growth. Remediation is the complete process: finding the moisture source, containing the work area, removing contaminated materials safely, cleaning and treating the space, drying it, and verifying with testing. Remediation is what actually solves the problem.
