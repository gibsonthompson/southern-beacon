Most people don't call us because they saw mold. They call because something felt off. A smell that won't leave. A spot that keeps coming back. A basement that's damp after every hard rain.

If any of these sound like your house, it's worth an hour of our time to look. If it turns out to be nothing, we'll tell you that too, and we won't charge you to hear it.

Talk it over with us
  1. A musty smell that keeps coming backEspecially in a basement, closet, or crawlspace, and worse after it rains.
  2. Dark spotting on walls, grout, or ceilingsAround windows, under sinks, near vents, or behind furniture on outside walls.
  3. Water in the basement after heavy rainEven a damp line along the base of the wall counts. It won't fix itself.
  4. Allergy or asthma flare-ups only at homeYou feel fine at work or on vacation, then it starts again when you get back.
  5. A home inspector flagged moisture"Evidence of water intrusion" on a report is a reason to look closer, not panic.
  6. Chalky white residue on block wallsThat's efflorescence, a sign water is moving through the foundation.

The mold is what you see. The water is why it's there.

Plenty of outfits will scrub a wall and send you a bill. Then it comes back, because the moisture that grew it never got dealt with. We work both ends of the same job, which is the only version that lasts.

Questions people ask before they call.

How soon can someone come out?+
Someone answers the phone at any hour. If water is actively coming in, we head your way as soon as we hang up. For inspections and waterproofing estimates we usually get you on the calendar within a day or two.
Is black mold as dangerous as people say?+
You can't tell a mold's species by its color. Some molds bother people with allergies or asthma more than others, and small children and older folks tend to feel it first. A lab test tells you what's actually growing, which is the only way to know whether it's a real health concern or just an ugly stain.
Can I just clean it myself with bleach?+
Spraying a patch of mold tends to send spores into the air and does nothing about the moisture feeding it, so it usually comes back. Handling it properly means sealing off the area, filtering the air, taking out what's affected, and fixing the leak or dampness underneath.
Will my insurance pay for it?+
Depends on the cause. A sudden event like a burst pipe is often covered. Slow leaks and groundwater usually aren't. Either way, we photograph and document everything we find, which is what your adjuster will want to see.
What does this usually cost?+
A small, contained job can be a few hundred dollars. Bigger jobs with demolition, drying, and lab testing run more. After we look at it you get a written, itemized number before any work starts, so there are no surprises.
How do I know it worked?+
We test the air after remediation and hand you the lab results. You end up with documentation showing the space came back to normal, not just our word that it's done.
After you waterproof, is the water gone for good?+
If the system is built for how water actually moves around your foundation, yes. That's why we start by figuring out where it's getting in before recommending anything, and the work comes with a written warranty.
